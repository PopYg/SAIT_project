Changing The World Through Creative Research

Graphene

SAIT developed separation membrane that can selectively control gas permeation using cross-layer thickness of graphene sheet. This will be available on new applications such as energy, environment. (Science, 2013)

Re-RAM

SAIT improved several problems of ReRAM including current density, temperature stability, cycling endurance and distribution. (Nature Communications, 2013)

In-situ Observation

SAIT identified the switching mechanisms of oxide-based resistance element using in-situ TEM analysis technology for the first time. We plan to apply this to ReRAM device development. (Nature Communications, 2013)

New Graphene transistor

Most recently, SAIT proposed and demonstrated a new logic device structure and published the results in the journal Science. According to the paper, the new graphene transistor opens up new opportunities in high speed transistor research. (Science, 2012)

Optical Sensor

SAIT developed an oxide semiconductor photosensor that will be used in future interactive displays such as smart TVs and electronic boards that communicate with a regular laser pointer. The outcome of this study is scientifically significant in recognizing the fundamental physical phenomenon of the photo-reaction of oxide semiconductor and proposes a high-speed operating mode. (Nature Materials, 2012)

Research directions for Graphene

SAIT has also proposed research directions for graphene electronics. In a Nature review paper, SAIT suggested device and processing technologies which will enable commercialization by integrating graphene with silicon technology. (Nature, 2011)

GaN LED on Glass

SAIT succeeded in realizing GaN LEDs by cultivating nearly single-crystalline GaN on amorphous glass substrates. The GaN single-crystallinity on glass was enabled by introducing a thin layer of titanium. This technology is anticipated to serve as the basis in applying GaN LEDs to flat, large-sized back-light units or illumination systems. (Nature Photonics, 2011)

ReRAM

SAIT proposed a new structure that dramatically increases the durability of ReRAM, which is being spotlighted as the next-generation of memory technology. It secured durability, enabling 1 trillion data recordings and erasures, and opening the possibility for commercialization. (Nature Materials, 2011)
